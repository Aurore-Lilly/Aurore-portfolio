Hike with me: Case study

For this project my client's goal was to create a healthier community, by giving their users a simpler way to schedule a hike with friends. But also offer an experience that is more fun than going to the gym.

Role: UX / UI Designer Course: Team Treehouse Techdegree

It was important that the app looks and feels intuitive, and that the user enjoys a swift and friendly experience while scheduling their hike. This is why it is so important to know who the user is, and what they are prioritizing in a health application

The process:

I started by conducting a card sorting excercise to help design an information architecture, workflow, menu structure or website navigation paths. It allowed me to think about how users would expect the content to be group on the application and which label might make more sense to them. It also helped me determinate how many potential categories there should be. Clike here to participate in the card sorting exercise.

Low Fidelity Wireframe:

Then I went on to sketching and iterating on the design of each frames. When I arrived to a result that made sense for the primary user Joaquim, I started to work on a low fridelity wireframe in Balsamiq. Joaquim will be swiftly brought to the scheduling screen once logged in. From there, he can view hiking trails and decides what type of trail he is looking for that day. Once he picks a trail, Joaquim can schedule and invite his friends. Finally, when the hike is completed he can share and rate the experience with the 'hike wth me' community.
